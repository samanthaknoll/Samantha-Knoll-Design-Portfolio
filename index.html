Instructional Design Process

My instructional design process consists of four key phases: Needs Analysis, Script and Storyboard, Development, and Evaluation. Learn more about my approach below.

Needs Analysis

A proper Needs Analysis includes engaging with key stakeholders to identify specific challenges and training gaps found via data analytics, surveys, and interviews. This process culminates in a detailed deliverable that outlines the identified problem areas, learner profiles, and recommended training solutions.

Script and Storyboard

In script writing, the focus is on crafting relatable stories and real-world scenarios that resonate with learners. Scripts are designed for scenario-based learning, immersing learners in practical situations. Storyboards and mock-ups are created to showcase the visual design, user-experience, and mobile responsiveness.

Development

In the development phase, storyboards are transformed into interactive and immersive eLearning experiences with the help of course authoring tools (Lectora, Storyline), native LMS elements, and video development software (Camtasia). Courses are delivered on schedule and fully customized with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Evaluation

The final stage of course development includes a comprehensive review to analyze customer-centric metrics such as learner satisfaction, enrollment, completion, and engagement. This review also incorporates performance indicators like assessment scores, knowledge retention, and post-course feedback. By analyzing these metrics, we aim to measure the ROI and overall quality of the learning experience.
